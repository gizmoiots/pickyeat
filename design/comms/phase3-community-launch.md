## The r/pune post

Reddit is the highest-conversion channel for Phase 3. Single post, posted Saturday morning (~9-10 AM IST), when r/pune traffic peaks.

**Title:** *I built a thing that picks what to order at any Pune restaurant — works in your browser*

**Body:**

```
Hi r/pune — first-time poster, regular lurker.

I've been working on something called pickyeat. The premise: open any
restaurant menu, point your phone at it, tell it what you're in the mood
for, get three dishes back with one-line reasons that actually make sense.

It scans the menu (Marathi or English), filters out anything you can't eat
(allergens, diet), and matches what you want against what diners actually
order there from public reviews.

Some examples from this week:

- Tried it at Vaishali. It picked the medu wada upma and the rava idli for
  brunch over the more obvious dosa, said "two reviews call the upma the
  Sunday-morning move." It was right.
- Tested with my flatmate who's strictly vegetarian. Filter tag handles it
  cleanly — no chicken biryani sneaking through.
- Translate mode is the killer feature for parents who can't read English
  menus at fancy places. Works on Marathi, Hindi, and a bunch of others.

Free, no signup, runs in any browser at pickyeat.com. Works on phone, tablet,
laptop — installs to home screen if you want.

A few things to know:

- It's brand new. ~20 friends have used it. If you find bugs I'd love to
  hear (DMs open or comment here).
- I deliberately did not paywall the basics. Diet filter and allergen filter
  are free, anonymous, no signup. If you ever see something gating those,
  yell at me.
- We don't sell your data. We don't profile you for advertisers. The
  privacy policy is at pickyeat.com/privacy and it's in plain English.

If you scan a place I haven't seen yet, you're literally adding it to the
app — every restaurant becomes free for the next person.

Try it on whatever you're eating today. Tell me what worked, what didn't.

— Nitin
```

**Reply patiently to every comment for 48 hours.** Specifically:

- "How is this different from Zomato?" — Zomato suggests *restaurants*. pickyeat suggests *what to order*. Different layer of the decision.
- "Does it work for [cuisine]?" — "Yes, scan it and let me know how it does. The more menus people scan, the better it gets at that cuisine."
- "Will this be paid eventually?" — "The core stays free. Premium ₹49/mo (or ₹499/yr) unlocks macro tracking, history, group mode over 4 people. Allergen filter, diet filter, the basic picks — those are free forever."
- "How are you handling data?" — Link to /privacy. Mention India-region storage.
- "I tried it and the picks were wrong at [place]" — "Send me the restaurant and what you actually ordered — that's exactly the feedback loop I need."

**Do not delete critical comments.** Engage with the harshest ones first.

**Title:** *What 200 Pune diners ordered this week — pickyeat data*

**Outline:**

1. Two-paragraph intro on why menu-ordering is a problem worth solving
2. Top 10 most-picked dishes across Pune this week (real data from the cache)
3. Most-picked dish per cuisine (north Indian / south Indian / continental / chinese)
4. Most "I ordered this" rate — dishes where the pick → order conversion was highest
5. A note on bias (only 200 users, only certain restaurants) so we don't overclaim
6. Quick plug for the app, link to install

SEO target: "best dishes in Pune", "what to order at [restaurant]". Weekly cadence — same template, fresh data.
